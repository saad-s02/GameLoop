# Chat workspace design: Plan My Night as a conversation (2026-07-20)

Approved by Saad on 2026-07-20 through the brainstorming flow. Four decisions were locked interactively before this document: build on a branch and demo the frozen polished build on 2026-07-21; Claude-style layout with the conversation beside a persistent plan panel; converge this branch with the completed Relive removal and the hybrid real-data integration; implement as a reskin over the existing stream with no backend contract changes.

## Why

Saad wants Plan My Night to feel like a conversation the user is having, modeled after Claude and ChatGPT, instead of a form with prompts and stacked panels. The backend already thinks in turns: delta extraction, deterministic merge, answerable clarifications, and a history thread shipped in the conversational build. This design gives that machinery the interface it implies. Separately, Saad wants the app as least simulated as possible, which the July 25 weekend research (research/2026-07-25-real-data/) makes concrete.

## Fiction stance

The venue and tonight's game stay fictional: Harbourview Arena, the replayed real-record hockey game, the simulated gates, stands, and seat map. This is what keeps lib/planning and lib/ai untouched. The real layer is data presented by the UI, never generated by the model: real restaurants near the arena and the real July 25 weekend transit conditions, carried in a reduced fixture with SNAPSHOT provenance and accessed dates. Claims about real businesses use the research evidence tiers (certified versus self-described versus friendly) and degrade honestly where evidence is absent (nut-free has no verified policies at any researched restaurant, and the UI says so). The NO_GEOGRAPHY rule in lib/ai/prompts.ts is unchanged because the model never sees or names the real places.

## Page architecture

/plan becomes a two-region workspace on desktop: the conversation on the left (message thread plus composer), the plan artifact panel on the right. The panel is the polished hero shipped in the 2026-07-19 polish pass, reused wholesale: outcome sentence, glyph timeline, constraint strip, diff badges, updating in place on refinements with the existing kept, replaced, and dropped grammar. The panel header carries the disruption quick actions. The panel footer carries the new real-places card. On mobile the panel stacks below the thread with a sticky jump control. The homepage stays chat-first, already reduced to the single Plan My Night destination by the Relive removal.

## Conversation model

The chat is composed from the existing SSE TraceEnvelope stream. No API, schema, or planning changes.

User turns: typed free text, or one of the four suggested prompts (the former demo chips). In demo mode the free-text composer stays honestly disabled with the existing copy, and the suggested prompts run the zero-LLM path exactly as the chips do today.

Assistant turns are composed from stream events:
- While streaming, the turn shows a typing indicator and a live Reasoning disclosure holding the decision log rows, open during the stream and folding to the Built from N signals summary when done. This is the exact component and collapse contract from the polish pass, relocated into the turn. The user-intent-only toggle behavior moves with it, along with its regression test.
- A party clarification renders as an assistant question bubble with the existing inline steppers and Use this action.
- An event mismatch renders as the existing honest redirect line at the top of the turn.
- Assumptions render as short first-person lines with their provenance, in the spirit of: I assumed the 18:15 Lakeshore West train; tell me if you are arriving differently.
- The narrative explanation text is the body of the assistant reply.
- plan_result closes the turn with a one-line confirmation echoing the hero sentence, and the panel updates.

Refinements ride the existing refinement path unchanged. The transcript itself replaces the What you have told us section, which is removed. Constraint adjusted events render inside the turn that caused them.

Error and stall states reuse the honest error treatment and Retry from the polish pass, rendered inside the affected assistant turn.

## Real-data layer

A new reduced fixture lib/data/real-nearby.json is authored from research/2026-07-25-real-data/candidates.json: name, rating, review count, walk minutes, price level, dietary evidence tags with the certified versus self-described versus friendly distinction, weekend evening hours flag, source URL, accessed date. A Zod schema validates it at the boundary like every other fixture. A loader lives with the other server-side loaders so nothing oversized enters the client bundle; the card receives only the filtered primitives it renders, following the eyebrow precedent from the polish pass.

When a plan lands, the panel footer shows Real places near the arena: two or three entries filtered by the plan's dietary constraints, each with rating, walk minutes, the evidence-tier line, and a SNAPSHOT badge with the accessed date. The card is labeled as coming from research notes, not from the planner, so real data never masquerades as model output. For nut-free the card renders the honest absence statement instead of options.

The July 25 weekend transit reality (Lakeshore West at 30 minute frequency for construction, UP Express replaced by GO buses) becomes a preset quick action named July 25 weekend service alongside the existing disruptions. This requires one new disruption definition in lib/planning/disruptions.ts and is the single planning-core addition in this design, built tests-first with exact fixtures like every deterministic-core change.

## Components and files

New: MessageThread, UserTurn, AssistantTurn, ChatComposer (absorbing FollowUpComposer logic and the suggested prompts), ReasoningDisclosure (the relocated log collapse), NearbyRealOptions, lib/data/real-nearby.json with schema and loader, and the July 25 service preset.

Reused: ItineraryTimeline, ConstraintsStrip, SkeletonTimeline (as the panel placeholder while the first plan builds), SourceBadge, the error and Retry treatment, all Lit Sheet tokens and motion rules.

Refactored: PlanClient.tsx (495 lines) splits into a PlanWorkspace orchestrator, the thread region, and the panel region, each small enough to hold in context alone.

Removed by the merged Relive branch: the Relive page, route, nav entry, recap warmup, and relive-only copy. Dead-code candidates listed in that branch's commit body await Saad's hand review separately (lib/ai recap exports stay untouched per the hand-review rule).

## What does not change

The POST /api/plan contract and SSE event schema. The deterministic merge and the planning core's existing behavior; the sole planning-core addition is the July 25 service disruption named above. The extraction and refinement prompts and everything in lib/ai. The zero-LLM demo guarantee and the poisoned-key webServer proof. The access gate. The Lit Sheet design system, the single reduced-motion block discipline, dashed exclusively for SIMULATED, provenance on every external value, AA floors.

## Testing

Vitest: unit tests for the turn-composition logic (stream events to turn segments), the real-nearby fixture schema, the dietary filtering with evidence tiers, and new copy functions. The collapse-contract component behavior keeps its coverage in the relocated ReasoningDisclosure.

Playwright: both smoke specs are rewritten to the chat selectors but keep the same flows and assertions: access, suggested prompt, streaming reasoning open then folded, hero in the panel, clarification answered inline in the thread, refinement with diff badges, disruption, the real-places card with an evidence line, reset. The poisoned-key webServer stays byte-identical. The reasoning collapse contract is asserted on both the fresh and replan paths as today.

Gates green at every commit. Sonnet implements from task briefs, opus reviews per wave, the same discipline as the polish pass.

## Risks

The e2e rewrite is the largest mechanical risk; it lands in the same commit as the layout so gates never go red between commits. The two-region layout needs a real mobile pass at 390px before review. Relocating the reasoning disclosure must not regress the toggle wedge fixed in the polish pass; its regression assertions move in the same commit. The real-places card must never read as a model recommendation; the label and the evidence tiers are the guard.

## Out of scope

A persisted first-class message model, multi-session chat history, and transcript sharing. A full real-city pivot that names the city or real venue in model output. Any lib/ai or lib/planning change. Rebuilding the anchor event around the real July 25 concert; tonight's game remains the replayed real-record hockey game.
